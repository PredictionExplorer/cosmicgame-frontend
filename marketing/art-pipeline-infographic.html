How the Art Is Made

Every Cosmic Signature NFT is generated by simulating the Three Body Problem -- three celestial bodies locked in gravitational orbit. Real physics. Spectral light. No AI. Click any stage to explore the details.

STAGE 01 Seed & RNG [+]

A hex seed (from the NFT's on-chain randomness) is fed into a SHA3-256 hash function to produce an infinite, deterministic stream of random numbers.

Hash Function
SHA3-256 (Keccak) -- cryptographic quality randomness with full avalanche effect. A single bit change in the seed produces completely different output.
Chaining
32-byte buffer extended by: hash(seed || previous_buffer). Produces billions of pseudorandom bytes from any seed.
Drives
Body masses, positions, velocities, color palettes, drift parameters, effect randomization -- every aspect of the art.
Source
src/sim.rs -- Sha3RandomByteStream
STAGE 02 Physics Simulation [+]

100,000 random three-body configurations are generated and simulated using Newtonian gravity with a 4th-order Yoshida symplectic integrator -- the same class of method used in astrophysics research.

Gravity
Classical Newtonian pairwise: a_i = -G * m_j * (r_i - r_j) / |r_i - r_j|^3, with G = 9.8
Integrator
Yoshida 4th-order symplectic -- preserves energy over millions of timesteps (unlike Euler or Runge-Kutta methods). Constants: W1 = 1.351, W0 = -1.702.
Body Parameters
Mass: 100-300 units. Position: ±300 per axis. Velocity: ±1 per axis. All in 3D.
Timestep
dt = 0.001. Per orbit: 1M warmup steps + 1M recorded steps = 2M total.
Source
src/sim.rs -- symplectic_step, get_positions
STAGE 03 Orbit Selection (Borda Search) [+]

From 100,000 simulated orbits, the system selects the single most visually interesting one using Borda-style rank aggregation across two quality metrics.

Fast Rejection
Discard orbits with: total energy > 10 (unbound), angular momentum < 10 (degenerate), or escaping bodies (kinetic > binding energy by threshold -0.3).
Chaos Metric
Per-body distance to the other two bodies' COM over time → FFT → std dev of magnitude spectrum. Lower = more chaotic. The Borda ranking rewards more chaotic orbits with higher points.
Equilateralness
Mean of (min_side / max_side) over time. 1.0 = perfect equilateral triangle. Favors balanced triangular shapes.
Borda Weights
Chaos: 0.75, Equilateralness: 11.0. Triangle balance dominates selection (~15x weight).
Parallelism
All 100K evaluations run in parallel via rayon (data-parallel threading).
Source
src/sim.rs -- select_best_trajectory; src/analysis.rs
STAGE 04 Camera Drift [+]

A slow camera motion is applied to all recorded positions, creating parallax and cinematic perspective shifts in the final video.

Elliptical (default)
Viewpoint traces an ellipse with configurable scale, arc fraction (0-1, how much of the orbit to traverse), and eccentricity (0-0.95). Parameters randomized per-seed.
Brownian
Random walk using Box-Muller Gaussian displacements at each timestep. Organic, wandering motion.
Linear
Constant-velocity translation. Simple slow pan across the scene.
Source
src/drift.rs, src/drift_config.rs
STAGE 05 Color Generation (OKLab) [+]

Each body is assigned a color sequence in the OKLab perceptual color space -- 120 degrees of hue separation ensures maximally distinct colors that evolve over time.

Color Space
OKLab (Bjorn Ottosson) -- perceptually uniform. Equal numerical distances = equal perceived color differences.
Hue Separation
120° between bodies + fixed phase offsets [0°, 120°, 240°] for maximum contrast.
Time Evolution
Base hue + log-time drift + sine wave (freq 1.8-4.0 Hz per seed) + random jitter. Chroma and lightness also modulated.
Alpha Variation
Per-body opacity shuffled from {1/13M, 1/15M, 1/17M} for subtle depth hierarchy.
Source
src/render/color.rs, src/render/constants.rs
STAGE 06 Spectral Rendering [+]

Instead of drawing in RGB, the renderer accumulates energy across 16 wavelength bins (380-700 nm) -- the full visible spectrum. One million timesteps of triangle edges are drawn with anti-aliased, velocity-dependent spectral light.

Spectral Bins
16 bins, 20 nm each, from 380 nm (deep violet) to 700 nm (far red). Each pixel stores a full spectral power distribution.
Line Drawing
Anti-aliased with Gaussian falloff: energy = α × exp(-d² / σ²). Fractional bin interpolation prevents spectral aliasing.
Velocity Thickness
Fast bodies → thin, bright streaks. Slow bodies → wider, softer trails. HDR boost up to 8x at close encounters.
Depth Effects
Circle-of-confusion (depth of field) + exponential depth fade: exp(-|z| × 0.002)
Spectral Shift
High-energy pixels undergo red-shift -- energy redistributed toward longer wavelengths for "heat glow" effect.
Chromatic Dispersion
Radial prismatic effect: each wavelength bin samples from slightly offset spatial positions, producing rainbow fringing near edges.
SPD to RGB
Bruton-style wavelength→RGB basis with per-bin tone weights. SIMD-accelerated on x86_64.
Source
src/render/drawing.rs, src/spectrum.rs, src/spectral_constants.rs
STAGE 07 Post-Processing & Output [+]

A curated chain of post-effects transforms the raw spectral render into a polished artwork. Two-pass histogram tonemapping ensures optimal exposure. Output: 16-bit PNG + 30-second H.265 video at 60 fps.

Tonemapping
AgX-style log/spline curve with matrix transforms. Two-pass histogram analysis for percentile-based exposure. Paper white: 0.92, highlight rolloff: 2.25.
Bloom & Glow
DoG bloom (difference-of-Gaussians), chromatic bloom with per-channel offsets, broad-radius glow.
Nebula
Multi-octave OpenSimplex2S noise (fBm-style) composited beneath trails. Per-seed density and coloring.
Color Grading
Cinematic grade: vibrance, clarity, tone curve, cool shadow tint, warm highlight tint, vignette.
Additional Effects
Perceptual blur (OKLab space), micro-contrast, opalescence, champlevé, aether scattering, atmospheric depth, edge luminance, fine texture.
Video Encoding
H.265, CRF 17, 10-bit color (yuv422p10le), 60 fps, ~30 seconds. Raw 16-bit frames piped to FFmpeg.
Image Output
16-bit PNG for maximum color fidelity (65,536 levels per channel vs 256 for 8-bit).
Source
src/render/effects.rs, src/render/video.rs, src/render/histogram.rs

Key Principles

Deterministic
Same seed = same art, every time. Verified by CI with SHA-256 hash comparison across builds. Your NFT's art is provably tied to its on-chain seed.
Physics-Based
Real Newtonian gravity and symplectic integration. The unpredictable beauty of the Three Body Problem is the artist -- not an AI model or random noise.
Spectral
16 wavelength bins (380-700 nm) capture the full visible spectrum. Color mixing happens physically, producing transitions impossible in standard RGB.
Open Source (CC0)
The entire codebase is public domain. Anyone can read the code, verify the algorithm, reproduce any NFT's art, or build on it.
