## Game Mechanic Deep-Dive Content Ideas

These educational threads and posts explain specific game mechanics with contract-accurate detail. Use across Weeks 2-12 on a rotating basis.

### Thread: "How the Dutch Auction Works" (8 tweets)

1. The price starts at 2x the previous round's first bid and declines linearly over ~2 days
2. Show a price curve visual (ceiling to floor over time)
3. After someone bids, ETH pricing switches to ~1% incremental steps
4. CST has its own Dutch auction: ~12-hour cycle, resets after each bid
5. CST bids are burned from supply. You still get 100 CST reward.
6. If you wait long enough, CST bids can be net-positive (burn < reward)
7. Random Walk NFT = 50% off any ETH bid (one-time per NFT)
8. The game's pricing is designed for fair market-based discovery, not insider advantages

### Thread: "Zero Creator ETH -- How Cosmic Signature Is Different" (6 tweets)

1. Compare: typical NFT project mints, sells, keeps proceeds
2. Cosmic Signature: 0% ETH to any team wallet. Verifiable on-chain.
3. Where does the ETH go? 25% main prize, 8% chrono, 4% raffle, 6% stakers, 7% Protocol Guild, ~50% rolls forward
4. The only non-participant allocation: CST tokens to marketing wallet (governed by DAO)
5. Even the 7% public goods allocation is enforced by smart contract code
6. This is what "value to participants" actually looks like

### Thread: "The Endurance Champion and Chrono-Warrior Explained" (7 tweets)

1. Two champion titles tracked in real-time every round
2. Endurance Champion: longest single unbroken streak as last bidder
3. It's the longest SINGLE stretch, not cumulative time. One 6-hour quiet period > five 1-hour stretches.
4. Chrono-Warrior: longest reign as Endurance Champion
5. When EC changes hands, the outgoing EC's "chrono interval" is measured
6. CW gets the most lucrative secondary prize: 8% of the round's total ETH
7. Strategy: bid during quiet periods. Timing beats capital.

### Thread: "The Growing Prize Pool Math" (5 tweets)

1. ~50% of ETH stays in the game after each round (only 50% distributed)
2. New bids add fresh ETH, but only half leaves
3. Worked example: Round 1 has 1 ETH, Round 2 starts with 0.5 + new bids, Round 3 even bigger
4. Anyone can donate ETH to the game at any time, growing the pool further
5. Early rounds seed later ones -- the game is designed to compound

### Thread: "CST: More Than a Governance Token" (6 tweets)

1. You earn 100 CST every time you bid. Guaranteed, no raffle needed.
2. CST bids are BURNED from supply -- deflationary pressure
3. If the CST Dutch auction declines low enough, your burn < 100 CST reward = net gain
4. CST powers the DAO: 100 CST to propose, 2-week voting, 3% quorum
5. The DAO can adjust prize percentages, timer settings, public goods recipient
6. Active bidders naturally accumulate governance power

### Thread: "How CSN Staking Yield Works" (5 tweets)

1. 6% of the game's ETH balance is sent to the staking contract every round
2. That ETH is split equally among ALL staked Cosmic Signature NFTs
3. Your reward accumulates until you unstake (you get ETH + your NFT back)
4. Each NFT can only be staked once ever -- choose your timing carefully
5. Math: 20 staked NFTs, 10 ETH round = 0.6 ETH / 20 = 0.03 ETH per NFT per round

### Thread: "How the Art Works -- The Three Body Problem" (8 tweets)

1. Every Cosmic Signature NFT is a visualization of the Three Body Problem -- three celestial bodies orbiting under real Newtonian gravity
2. Unlike two bodies (neat ellipses), three bodies produce fundamentally chaotic trajectories. Tiny differences in starting conditions create wildly different paths. Deterministic chaos.
3. For each NFT, the system generates 100,000 random three-body configurations and simulates them using a 4th-order Yoshida symplectic integrator (the same class of method used in astrophysics research)
4. Each orbit is scored on two metrics: orbital complexity (via FFT analysis of body distances -- more chaotic = more points) and equilateralness (how balanced the triangle stays). A Borda rank aggregation selects the single most visually interesting orbit.
5. The winning orbit is rendered in the SPECTRAL domain -- not RGB. 16 wavelength bins from 380 nm (deep violet) to 700 nm (far red). Real physics of light, not just color codes.
6. Every timestep, the three bodies form a triangle. The edges are drawn with anti-aliased, velocity-dependent lines. Fast encounters = thin bright streaks. Slow passages = wide soft trails. Depth of field blurs distant elements.
7. Then: AgX tonemapping (from professional film pipelines), bloom, nebula clouds (OpenSimplex noise), cinematic color grading, chromatic dispersion. The full post-production suite.
8. Same seed = same art, every time. No AI. No neural networks. Pure physics and spectral optics. CC0 open source -- verify it yourself.

### Thread: "Behind the Renderer -- Spectral Light in 16 Bins" (6 tweets)

1. Most digital art works in RGB: three channels (red, green, blue). Cosmic Signature works in the spectral domain: 16 channels spanning the entire visible spectrum (380-700 nm).
2. Why? When you mix colors spectrally, you get physically correct transitions. Violet + red doesn't just become magenta -- the energy flows through the actual wavelength bins between them.
3. Each pixel stores a spectral power distribution (SPD) -- 16 energy values, one per 20 nm band. After one million timesteps of trail accumulation, this SPD is converted to visible color via Bruton-style basis functions.
4. The renderer also does: velocity-dependent HDR (up to 8x boost at close encounters), depth of field from z-coordinates, exponential depth fade, and energy-based spectral red-shifting for "heat glow" in intense regions.
5. Plus radial chromatic dispersion: each wavelength bin samples from slightly offset positions, like a prism separating light. This produces rainbow fringing near edges -- physically motivated, not a filter.
6. The result is color mixing and luminance behavior that is impossible to achieve with standard RGB rendering. Physics as an artist.

### Thread: "One Seed, One Universe" (5 tweets)

1. Each Cosmic Signature NFT has a unique hex seed stored permanently on-chain. This seed is the ONLY input to the art generation pipeline. Everything else follows deterministically.
2. The seed drives a SHA3-256 random number generator that produces masses, positions, velocities, color palettes, camera drift parameters, and post-effect settings. One seed = one complete universe of decisions.
3. Same seed always produces the same image and video, pixel for pixel. This is verified in CI by SHA-256 hash comparison across different builds. Your NFT's art is provably tied to its on-chain seed.
4. The entire codebase is CC0 (public domain). Anyone can download the code, run any seed, and independently verify that the output matches. True on-chain provenance.
5. Different seeds produce dramatically different art: different orbital shapes, different color palettes, different visual effects. 100,000 orbits evaluated, the best one chosen. Every piece is curated by physics.
