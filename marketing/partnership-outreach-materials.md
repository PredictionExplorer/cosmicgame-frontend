## 6. Protocol Guild and Public Goods Partnership Outreach

```
Subject: Cosmic Signature -- 7% of Every Round Hardcoded to Protocol Guild

Hi Protocol Guild team,

We're reaching out to let you know that Cosmic Signature, a strategy game
launching on Arbitrum, has designated Protocol Guild as the recipient of
our public goods allocation.

How it works: 7% of the game contract's total ETH balance is automatically
transferred to your address when claimMainPrize() executes at the end of every
round. This is enforced by the charityEthDonationAmountPercentage parameter
(default: 7) in the smart contract -- not a pledge, not a promise, just code
that runs every round. The transfer is non-reverting: if it fails for any
reason, the round still completes normally (player prizes are never blocked).

Protocol Guild will be our public goods recipient for at least the first
year. The more successful the game becomes, the more ETH flows to
Ethereum's core contributors.
```

**Smart contract identifiers (technical alignment):** In source code, `CharityWallet` is the public goods recipient wallet, and `charityEthDonationAmountPercentage` configures the 7% allocation. The game's `charityAddress` points to our `CharityWallet` contract, which then forwards ETH to the actual recipient address. Use "public goods" language in all external messaging.

**Why this matters compared to other NFT projects:** Most NFT projects pledge charity donations but have no enforcement mechanism. Cosmic Signature hardcodes the 7% into every round's prize distribution logic. The percentage and recipient are adjustable only by the contract owner (intended to be the DAO), and only during inactive round stages. There is no way to skip or reduce the allocation within a round -- it executes automatically. Additionally, zero ETH goes to any team/creator wallet. The only non-participant allocation is CST tokens (not ETH) to a marketing wallet -- and that wallet's treasurer is appointed by the DAO.
